Reproductive Rights in Middle TN


UN Sustainability Goal #5: "Achieve gender equality and empower all women and girls."

Target 5.6: "Ensure universal access to sexual and reproductive health and reproductive rights as agreed in accordance with the Programme of Action of the International Conference on Population and Development and the Beijing Platform for Action and the outcome documents of their review conferences."

What is this site for?

The purpose of this site is to provide information about the history and current state of Reproductive Rights in Middle Tennesse as well provide links to resources and organizations that support the cause.

By educating visitors on the topic, people can better understand the situation as a whole and how it could relate to them as individuals. By provided visitors with resources and organizations supporting the cause, the passionate among them can further fight for Reproducetive Rights in Middle Tennessee.

What is the history of Reproductive Rights in Middle Tennessee?

The history of Reproductive Rights in Middle Tennessee date's all the way back to the 1930s with the opening of the first birth control clinic in Nashville.

The History section of the site goes into more detail about the major events of the timeline of Reproductive Rights.

How are things now?

In short, with the overturning of Roe v. Wade in 2022, Tennessee's trigger law banning abortion came into effect, making all abortions (with few exceptions) a Class C Felony for the person performing the operation.

The Current State section goes into more detail about this. It also includes an interactive map for getting a glance of current abortion laws in all 50 states.

What can I do about this?

You can donate to or volunteer at any of the Non-Profits listed on this site. Many of these organizations operate solely off of donations and volunteers, so it's vital to their function that they get the support they need.

With that, there are also some Actions that individuals can take to help out. With Education being the most important one, you can also do things like contacting your local government to share your ideas.
